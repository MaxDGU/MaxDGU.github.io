I am a computer scientist studying learning. Currently, I am a fully-funded graduate student at Princeton, where I work in the CoCoSci lab. Previously, I spent some time in Germany, working at the University of Tübingen on social network dynamics with the Computational Pragmatics Lab and at the Center for Humans and Machines in Berlin, studying the behavior and perception of Large Language Models in interactive online experiments. Prior to a 3-year stint in software engineering, I received my Bachelor’s degree in mathematics from Columbia University, where I worked on neural network uncertainty quantification in cancer research at the Azizi Lab.
